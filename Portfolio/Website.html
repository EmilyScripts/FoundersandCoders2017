How I Got Into Programming

My journey into programming is a relatively new one. In August I was looking into finding a new hobby or activity that would spark my interest. After looking at a few options but nothing really sparking my interest, I looked into coding and had a lookover at the subreddit /r/learnprogramming. After reading up a bit about it, I decided to look at some of the recommeneded resources for beginners, which led me to FreeCodeCamp. From here I instantly knew that this was what I was going to take up, the small exercise bites gave me lots of oppotunities for problem solving and became a highly enjoyable and rewarding experience. While doing a bit of the HTML and CSS course, I decided to look up more information about programming as a whole and find some books to read on the subject. It was here I realised how muc hcreativity there was in programming and I soon after realised that this was something I was serious about and began looking at courses that were on offer that could help with my learning process.

Why Founders and Coders

While looking around for programming courses located in London, one thing became immediately clear, I was going to need to find a sudden influx of cash. I desperately wanted to go to a face to face course but every course I looked at was massively too expensive. Here, I started to instead look at online courses that were recommended on Udemy so I started The Web Developer Bootcamp. This further clarified that I was serious about programming so I checked to see if there were any cheaper evening courses I could aim to apply for. It was this that lead me to an article by The Guardian about Founders and Coders.
